Beef Wellington - a Ramsy signature

Beef Wellington is a classic dish that epitomizes gourmet dining. This recipe involves a succulent beef tenderloin coated with a rich mushroom duxelles, wrapped in a delicate puff pastry, and baked to golden perfection. The combination of flavors and textures makes it an exquisite centerpiece for any special occasion.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds beef tenderloin
  • 2 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh mushrooms
  • 1/2 cup pâté
  • 1 puff pastry sheet, thawed
  • 1 egg yolk, beaten
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 1/2 cup red wine
  • 1/2 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup water

Directions

  1. Preheat oven to 450 degrees F (230 degrees C).
  2. Place beef in a baking dish. Spread with 2 tablespoons softened butter. Bake for 10 to 15 minutes or until browned. Remove from pan and allow to cool completely. Keep drippings in the pan.
  3. Melt 2 tablespoons butter in a skillet over medium heat. Sauté mushrooms for 5 minutes. Remove from heat and allow to cool.
  4. Mix pâté and mushrooms together, and season with salt and pepper. Spread mixture over beef. Roll out pastry dough and place beef in the center. Fold up and seal all the edges, making sure the seams are not too thick. Brush with egg yolk.
  5. Place beef in a 9x13-inch baking dish and bake for 10 to 15 minutes or until pastry is golden brown.
  6. While beef is baking, place all reserved drippings, beef broth, and red wine in a small saucepan. Cook over high heat until thickened, then strain. Serve as a sauce with the sliced beef.
